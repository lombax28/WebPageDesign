· Action Video Games ·

Action Video Games usually fill in the following requirements:

  • Require the player to have hand-eye coordination
  • Require the player to have a good reaction time
  • Typically require the player to complete a certain amount of levels
  • There is a main character played, generally being the protagonist
  • At the end of a set of levels, there is usually a boss (hard to kill enemy, sometimes a harder version of a regular enemy) battle
  • There is a health and lives system (health is deducted upon being attacked, lives are deducted upon losing all health)

Scientific facts about Action Video Games:

  • Scientific studies have shown that people playing action video games have shown a 20% improvement in eye examinations than those that play a game like tetris.
